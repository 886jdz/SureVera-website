What SureVera Does

SureVera is a mobile app (iOS and Android) that shows consumers restaurant health inspection grades from official government sources. Users search for any restaurant and instantly see its most recent health score, grade, and violation details — sourced directly from the local health department.

The service is funded by a modest consumer subscription of $20/year. There is no cost to health departments.

Why This Matters

  • The CDC estimates 48 million Americans get foodborne illness each year
  • 128,000 hospitalizations and 3,000 deaths annually from foodborne disease
  • Most outbreaks are linked to restaurants and food service establishments
  • Health departments conduct inspections, but the results are often buried in PDFs or hard-to-navigate portals that consumers never see

The Gap We're Trying to Close

Health inspectors do critical work, but the data they produce rarely reaches the people who need it most — the consumer standing on a street corner deciding where to eat. SureVera bridges that gap by putting official health grades in consumers' pockets at the moment of decision.

What Your Department Gets

  • Extended reach: Your inspection data reaches consumers who would never visit your website or data portal
  • Official attribution: Every restaurant listing credits your department as the official data source
  • Fewer records requests: Consumers can self-serve through the app instead of filing individual FOIA requests
  • Modern mobile interface: A polished, mobile-first experience for your data at no cost to your department
  • Public health impact: Informed consumers reward compliant restaurants and incentivize better food safety practices

Data Formats We Support

We are extremely flexible and can work with whatever your systems produce:

  • Open data portals (Socrata, ArcGIS, CKAN)
  • CSV or Excel exports
  • PDF inspection reports
  • LIVES standard feeds
  • Custom APIs or database exports
  • Even emailed spreadsheets on a schedule

Current Coverage

SureVera currently covers 90+ cities across 20+ states, including major metros like New York City, Chicago, San Francisco, Seattle, Las Vegas, San Antonio, and many more. We are actively expanding coverage and prioritize cities where departments are willing to partner.

General Outreach Tips
10 best practices ▼
1
Personalize every email. Replace all brackets with real names, cities, and details. Generic emails get ignored.
2
Research before you write. Check their website, find the right contact person, and reference their specific dataset or portal by name.
3
Keep it short. Government employees are busy. If your email is longer than 2 scrolls on a phone, trim it.
4
Attach the one-pager. Always include the partnership PDF when relevant.
5
Follow up, but don't pester. 2 weeks, then 4 weeks, then move on. Three touches maximum.
6
Be genuinely grateful. These people protect public health for a living. Respect that.
7
Track everything. Keep a spreadsheet: department, contact name, date sent, response, next step.
8
Start with friendly departments. Cities that already have Socrata/ArcGIS portals believe in open data. Start there.
9
One big win opens doors. Getting one major city to add violations to their feed becomes social proof for the next pitch.
10
Consider the timing. Avoid budget season (Sept–Nov for most governments) and holidays. January–March and June–August are usually best.
